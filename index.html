Mădălina Zgreabăn

Second-year PhD candidate in NLP, Utrecht University

I research generalizability and explainability in NLU, with a focus on reasoning, benchmarks and theorem-prover–based training for (hopefully) improved model explanations. I am supervised by Lasha Abzianidze, Tejaswini Deoskar, and Yoad Winter.

I hold an MA in linguistics from Utrecht University and a BA in literature & linguistics from the University of Bucharest. Given my background, I’ve had many research 'lives'—from language (and vision) models to comedy roasts, weak definites, and critical literary theory and (word) literature :).

I love transdisciplinary research at the intersection of linguistics, reasoning, and AI, and organizing outreach events to encourage research and support others to get affordable education.
