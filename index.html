Mădălina Zgreabăn (b. 1998) is a second-year PhD candidate in NLP at Utrecht University. She researches generalizability and explainability in natural language understanding, with a focus on reasoning, benchmarks, and theorem-prover-based training for improved model explanations. She is supervised by Lasha Abzianidze, Tejaswini Deoskar, and Yoad Winter.

She holds a BA in literature and linguistics from the University of Bucharest and an MA in linguistics from Utrecht University. Her research path has ranged from language (and vision) models to comedy roasts, weak definites, and critical literary theory.

She enjoys transdisciplinary research at the intersection of linguistics, reasoning, and AI, and organizing outreach events that support accessible education.
