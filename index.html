Mădălina Zgreabăn

Second-year PhD candidate in NLP, Utrecht University

I research generalizability and explainability in NLU, with a focus on reasoning, benchmarks and theorem-prover–based training for (hopefully) improved model explanations. I am supervised by Lasha Abzianidze, Tejaswini Deoskar, and Yoad Winter.

With a background in literature, linguistics, and computational linguistics, I’ve had many research lives—from language (and vision) models to comedy roasts, weak definites, and literature :).

I love transdisciplinary research at the intersection of linguistics, reasoning and AI, and I also organize outreach events to encourage research and support others to get affordable education.
